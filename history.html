The Sacred History of Pithapuram

Tracing the divine origins and living legacy of Sri Kshetra Pithapuram — the earthly abode where Lord Sripada Srivallabha took His first avatar in Kaliyuga.

Pithapuram — The Sacred Cradle

Pithapuram (formerly known as Pithikapuram) is one of the most revered pilgrimage centres in Andhra Pradesh. It is consecrated as the birthplace of Lord Sripada Srivallabha — the first complete and perfect incarnation of Lord Dattatreya in the current age of Kaliyuga.

The ancient texts and the sacred scripture Sripada Srivallabha Charitamrutam narrate in profound detail how the Lord chose this spiritually potent land — blessed by sages, Siddhas, and rishis across the ages — as the site of His divine manifestation. The town sits on the banks of the Suvarnamukhi river and is held sacred by followers of the Dattatreya tradition across the world.

The Mahasamsthanam stands as a beacon of spiritual solace and cultural preservation, drawing pilgrims from across India and beyond who seek the blessings of the Lord. The temple complex has been thoughtfully maintained under the stewardship of the Andhra Pradesh Endowments Department, ensuring the sanctity and accessibility of the sacred premises for every devotee.

The Divine Incarnation

According to the sacred Charitamrutam, Lord Sripada Srivallabha was born into the noble Apasthamba Sutra Vaidiki Brahmin family of Sri Appala Raju Sharma and Smt. Sumati Maharani of Pithapuram. His birth was heralded by celestial portents — the sound of divine instruments, showers of flowers, and the appearance of enlightened sages who recognised the Avatara.

From the earliest years of His life, the Lord displayed miraculous qualities — mastery of all scriptures, the power to heal the sick, grant liberation to the dying, and manifest blessings in forms beyond human comprehension. He initiated countless disciples and revealed deep Vedantic truths to seekers who came from every corner of Bharata.

The Lord is said to have departed from Pithapuram to Kuruvapuram (in present-day Karnataka), where He resides to this day in His subtle form, continuously blessing devotees. The Swayambhu Padukas (divine footprints) enshrined at the Mahasamsthanam remain the physical focal point of His living presence for pilgrims and worshippers.

Historical Timeline

~14th Century CE

Birth of the Lord at Pithapuram

Lord Sripada Srivallabha incarnates in Pithapuram (Pithikapuram) as the son of Sri Appala Raju Sharma and Smt. Sumati Maharani. The Charitamrutam records this as the most auspicious event of the Kaliyuga.

Early Life

Divine Leelas & Initiation of Disciples

The Lord performs numerous miracles — raising the dead, curing incurable illness, granting liberation at the point of death, and teaching Advaita Vedanta to seekers. He initiates key disciples including Shankar Bhatt, through whom the Charitamrutam is narrated.

Before His Departure

Swayambhu Padukas Enshrined

Before departing to Kuruvapuram, the Lord leaves His divine footprints (Swayambhu Padukas) at Pithapuram as a permanent, living form for devotees. These Padukas continue to be the primary object of veneration and are believed to fulfil the sincere prayers of pilgrims.

Medieval Period

Spread of the Dattatreya Tradition

The legacy of Lord Sripada spreads across the Deccan through the work of His disciples. Pithapuram emerges as a major centre of the Dattatreya sampradaya, attracting saints, scholars, and seekers from across the subcontinent.

Modern Era

Mahasamsthanam Established

The Sripada Srivallabha Mahasamsthanam is formally established and placed under the administration of the Andhra Pradesh Endowments Department, ensuring systematic management of the temple's resources, daily rituals, Nithya Annadanam, and infrastructure.

Present Day

A Living Pilgrimage Centre

Pithapuram today receives hundreds of thousands of pilgrims annually. The Mahasamsthanam conducts daily Abhishekam, Kumkuma Archana, Annadanam, and multiple special sevas throughout the year. Digital infrastructure continues to be developed to serve the global community of devotees.

The Sacred Temple Complex

footprint

Swayambhu Padukas

The self-manifested divine footprints of Lord Sripada, the most sacred object in the temple, believed to radiate the living spiritual power of the Lord for all devotees who approach with sincerity.

restaurant

Nithya Annadanam

Every day without exception, free meals are offered to all pilgrims and visitors, continuing the Lord's own tradition of universal compassion and the principle that no devotee should leave the sacred precinct hungry.

grass

Goshala

The temple maintains a Goshala (cow sanctuary) in accordance with the Vedic tradition of reverence for the cow. Panchagavya from the Goshala is used in the daily Abhishekam rituals of the Lord.
