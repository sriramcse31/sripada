Goseva

Cow Care & Worship

The Goshala at the Mahasamsthanam is home to sacred cows cared for with love and reverence. Goseva — the service and worship of cows — is one of the most meritorious acts a devotee can perform in this age. Your contribution directly supports their daily care, nourishment, and ritual worship.

The sacred scriptures, including the Skanda Purana and Srimad Bhagavatam, speak extensively of the glory of Goseva. The cow is considered the earthly form of Kamadhenu — the divine wish-fulfilling cow — and is associated with Lord Vishnu, Goddess Lakshmi, and all the devatas. Serving the cow is said to please all the gods simultaneously.

Lord Krishna himself is known as Gopala — the protector of cows — and his divine leelas are inseparable from Goshala life. In the Mahabharata, gifting or caring for a cow is described as among the greatest of all donations (dana), capable of removing sins and granting liberation.

At the Sripada Srivallabha Mahasamsthanam, each cow in the Goshala is given a name, fed with love, and worshipped daily. Participating in Gopooja on auspicious days — especially Krishnashtami — is believed to bring peace, prosperity, and the grace of Lord Dattatreya to the donor's household for generations.
