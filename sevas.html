Abhishekam

Panchamurta Abhisheka Sahita Ashotottra Pooja

schedule 9:00 a.m.

Lord Dattatreya is the embodiment of 'Trimurthis'. Abhishekams with Panchamrutham (Cow Milk, Cow Curd, Cow Ghee, Honey and Sugar) are performed. As the Lord is fond of Abhishekams, Rudra Namaka Chamakam is also recited.

The first incarnation of Dattatreya is Sripada Srivallabha. By performing this Seva, Devotees are blessed with the Lord's Anugraha and their desires and wishes will be fulfilled.

Rudrabhishekam

Mahanyasapoorvaka Ekadasa Rudrabhisha Sahita Ashtottara Pooja

schedule 9:00 a.m.

The Lotus feet of the lord are worshipped with eleven items (Cow Milk, Cow Curd, Cow Ghee, Honey, Sugar, Sandal Wood, Vibhuthi, Perfume, Rosewater, Fruit Juice and Vattiverllu) 11 times along with Rudranamaka chamakams.

Of the 'Thrimurthis', Rudra is fond of Abhishekam. Lord Sripada Srivallabha is the incarnation of Dattatreya — so this Abhishekam is very much liked by him. All Devotees are requested to take part and receive his blessings in plenty.

Festival calendar_month Margasira Purnima

Datta Jayanti

Birthday of Lord Dattatreya — the most auspicious day of the year

Celebrated on the full moon day of Margasira (Nov–Dec), Datta Jayanti marks the divine incarnation of Lord Dattatreya. Grand Abhishekams, Homams, and Pallaki Seva are performed with great devotion. Devotees from across Andhra Pradesh converge at the Mandir.

Festival calendar_month Bhadrapada Bahula Panchami

Sripada Srivallabha Aradhana

Sacred Aradhana of the first incarnation of Dattatreya

The Aradhana of Lord Sripada Srivallabha falls on the 5th day of the dark fortnight of Bhadrapada (Aug–Sep). Special poojas and Abhishekams are performed to the holy Padukas and Prasadam is distributed to all devotees.

Festival event_repeat Monthly — Chitta Star

Chitta Nakshatram

Monthly birth star day of Lord Sripada Srivallabha

Chitta (Chitra) Nakshatram is the birth star of Lord Sripada Srivallabha. Every month on this auspicious day, special Abhishekams, Alankaram, and Rajita Pallaki Seva are performed. Devotees observe fasts and offer special prayers.

Festival calendar_month Ashadha Purnima

Guru Purnima

Festival of the Divine Guru — Dattatreya worshipped as Adi Guru

On the full moon of Ashadha (Jul), devotees pay homage to the divine lineage of Gurus. Lord Dattatreya, revered as the Adi Guru, is worshipped with Rudrabhishekam, Maha Homam, and Deeparadhana through the night.

Festival calendar_month Magha Bahula Chaturdashi

Maha Shivaratri

Night of Lord Shiva — Rudrabhishekam & Homam through the night

As Dattatreya is the embodiment of the Trimurthis — Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva — Maha Shivaratri is observed with great reverence. Ekadasa Rudrabhishekam and Maha Mruthyumjaya Homam are performed through the night.

Festival calendar_month Chaitra Shukla Navami

Sri Rama Navami

Birth anniversary of Lord Sri Rama — Kalyanam & Archana

Sri Rama Navami is celebrated with Sri Sita Rama Kalyanam, Archana, and Suprabhatam at the Mandir. Special Prasadam is prepared and distributed to all visiting devotees as part of the day's celebrations.

Festival calendar_month Chaitra Shukla Prathama

Ugadi

Telugu New Year — Panchanga Sravanam & special Abhishekam

Ugadi marks the start of the Telugu New Year. Panchanga Sravanam (astrological almanac reading) is conducted followed by special Abhishekam and Alankaram to the Padukas. Devotees receive Ugadi Pachadi as Prasadam.

Festival calendar_month Sravana Shukla Friday

Varalakshmi Vratam

Sacred vow to Goddess Varalakshmi for blessings & prosperity

Varalakshmi Vratam is observed by devotees seeking wealth, health, and well-being. Special Lakshmi Puja and Homam are performed at the Mandir with Kumkuma Archana and Prasadam distribution to all.

Festival calendar_month Sravana Bahula Ashtami

Krishna Janmashtami

Birth anniversary of Lord Sri Krishna — midnight Abhishekam

Krishna Janmashtami is celebrated at midnight with devotional singing, Bhajanas, and special Abhishekam. Dattatreya's connection to the Vishnu aspect of the Trimurthis makes this a significant observance at the Mandir.

Festival calendar_month Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi

Vinayaka Chavithi

Festival of Lord Ganesha — remover of all obstacles

Vinayaka Chavithi is celebrated with special Ganapati Abhishekam, Modaka Neivedyam, and Lakshmi Ganapati Homam. Devotees seek the blessings of Lord Ganesha to remove obstacles from their lives before undertaking new endeavours.

Festival calendar_month Ashwija Shukla 1–9

Navaratri

Nine nights of the Divine Mother — Devi Puja & Chandi Homam

Navaratri is observed over nine sacred nights with daily Devi Puja, Chandi Homam, and Kumkuma Archana. On Vijayadasami (the 10th day), special Ayudha Puja and Saraswati Puja are performed with great devotion.

Festival calendar_month Ashwija Shukla Dasami

Vijayadasami

Day of Victory — Ayudha Puja & Saraswati Archana

Vijayadasami marks the victory of righteousness over evil. Special sevas, Saraswati Puja, and Ayudha Puja are performed. New students traditionally begin their learning on this auspicious day with a prayer to the Lord.

Festival calendar_month Karthika Purnima

Karthika Purnima

Tripuri Purnima — Akhanda Deeparadhana through the night

Karthika Purnima is one of the most sacred days in the year. Akhanda Deeparadhana — continuous lamp offering — is performed through the night. Devotees float diyas on water and offer prayers to the Lord.

Festival event_repeat Fortnightly — Trayodasi

Pradosha Vratam

Bi-monthly observance — Rudrabhishekam at twilight

Pradosha Vratam is observed on the 13th tithi (Trayodasi) of each lunar fortnight. Special Rudrabhishekam is performed at Pradosha time (twilight). Devotees who participate are blessed with health, longevity, and the removal of doshas.

Festival event_repeat Trayodasi on Saturday

Sani Trayodasi

Special Abhishekam when Trayodasi falls on a Saturday

When Trayodasi tithi coincides with a Saturday, it is an especially powerful occasion. Panchamruta Abhisheka Sahita Ashtottara Pooja is performed at Pradosha Samaya (5:00 p.m.) with the devotee's name and Gothram.

Festival calendar_month Ashwija Bahula Amavasya

Deepavali

Festival of Lights — Akhanda Deepam & special Alankaram

Deepavali is celebrated at the Mandir with Akhanda Deepam, special Alankaram, and Lakshmi Puja. Rows of lamps illuminate the Mandir premises and devotees offer prayers for prosperity and the removal of darkness.

Festival calendar_month Margasira Shukla Ekadasi

Vaikunta Ekadasi

Most sacred Ekadasi — Vaikunta Dwara opens at dawn

Vaikunta Ekadasi is the most sacred of all Ekadasi days. Devotees fast and observe a night vigil, believing the gates of Vaikunta open at dawn. Special Abhishekam and Suprabhatam are performed before sunrise.

Festival calendar_month January 14

Makara Sankranti

Sun's northward journey — Uttarayana Punyakalam celebrations

Makara Sankranti marks the Sun's entry into Capricorn and the start of Uttarayana. Special Abhishekam and Til Neivedyam are performed. Devotees take a ritual bath before dawn and offer prayers for the new solar cycle.

Festival calendar_month Ashwija Shukla Navami

Maha Navami

The ninth day of Navaratri — Durga Puja & Chandi Parayanam

Maha Navami is the ninth day of Navaratri, dedicated to Goddess Durga in her most powerful form. Chandi Parayanam, Kumkuma Archana, and Devi Homam are conducted. Ayudha Puja is performed on the preceding evening.

Festival calendar_month Chaitra Shukla Purnima

Hanuman Jayanti

Birth anniversary of Lord Hanuman — Sundara Kanda Parayanam

Hanuman Jayanti is observed with Sundara Kanda Parayanam, special Abhishekam, and Hanuman Chalisa recitation. Devotees fast and participate in night-long Bhajanas. Vada Mala and Pancha Kashaya Abhishekam are the signature offerings on this day.
